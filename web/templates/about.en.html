About us

CepespData is an online platform that makes available the Brazilian electoral data. CepespData was developed by the Getulio Vargas Foundation’s Center for Politics and Economics of the Public Sector Studies (Cepesp/FGV) with the financial support from the São Paulo State Foundation for Research (FAPESP) to the project The Subnational Political Institutions: A Comparative Study of the Brazilian States (process number: 2013/15658-1) and with institutional support from the Brazilian Superior Electoral Court (TSE).

The Brazilian electoral data is originally made available through the TSE online repository. The documentation on the data cleaning procedures can be accessed on Cepesp public repository on GitHub (https://github.com/Cepesp-Fgv), which guarantees data integrity, transparency and collaboration with all users.

Methodological Note

Electoral results found in CEPESPData are based on TSE’s electoral section (polling station) data files. This aggregation level was chosen as our base level given they are the only TSE files that are not altered after post-electoral court decisions. Consequently, these files are the ones that best reflect the voters’ will and what occurred on election day. This implies slight differences between electoral results analysis with CepespData/FGV and with TSE data aggregated by municipality or electoral zone. In addition, our team has run analysis to guarantee that differences between CepespData/FGV and TSE published results for the electoral section aggregation level never exceeds 1%, ensuring the consistency of our data.
